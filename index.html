Tiananmen Square Massacre

Despite what you may hear, it happened.


This is a man known as Tank Man. He was a regular Chinese citizen in 1989, when the Tiananmen Square Massacre happened. He saw the soldiers in the square, and went up to them, standing in front of them and making the stop sign. He was seen speaking to the crew of one of the tanks momentarily, but the government quickly shut off the news broadcast and the fate of Tank Man is unknown. However, given the high death toll at the massacre, it is likely that he was killed or imprisoned.


This is the Tianamen Square Protest. Millions of people, the majority of which were students, packed themselves into Tiananmen Square in Beijing, China. They demanded political freedom in China. After the government refused to listen, their protest soon became a hunger strike. Days later, the government was still refusing to meet with the protestors, so ordinary men and women from Beijing began to bring them food to support their movement. After unsuccessful talks with government officials, 200,000 soldiers converged on the square. Nobody knows what happened next, as news broadcasts cut out and the Chinese government denies that the protests ever happened.
